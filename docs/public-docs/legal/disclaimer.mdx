## 2. Hypothetical and Simulated Outputs

Backtests, simulations, model outputs, optimization results, and comparative
analytics are hypothetical.

They are generated from historical data, assumptions, parameters, and model
logic. They do not represent actual trading and should not be presented or
interpreted as actual achieved performance.

Actual outcomes can differ materially from hypothetical or simulated results.

---

## 3. Important Limitations of Backtests and Simulations

Hypothetical results may fail to capture or may understate important real-world
factors, including:

- data quality issues, missing data, stale data, or survivorship bias
- commissions, fees, funding, borrowing costs, taxes, and other transaction costs
- slippage, spread variation, market impact, and liquidity constraints
- latency, outages, routing differences, rejected orders, and venue-specific behavior
- operational failures, human error, and execution process differences
- changing market structure, regime shifts, and model instability over time

Because of these limitations, a strong historical result does not mean a
strategy will perform similarly in any live or future environment.

## 4. No Guarantee of Future Results

Past performance, simulated performance, and hypothetical performance are not
indicative of future results.

A profitable backtest, favorable simulation, or strong comparative report does
not mean that a strategy will be profitable, reliable, suitable, or safe under
current or future market conditions.
